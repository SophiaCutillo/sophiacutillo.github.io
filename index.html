01

Social Media Strategy & Brand Growth

Marketing Internship Emilie Buck Lewis Studios Project Manager 3 Months · Team of 4 to 5
+
Project Overview
An independent artist selling work online wanted to grow her audience and increase sales. With limited time and low enthusiasm for social media, the goal was to build a strategy that was effective, realistic, and sustainable for a reluctant content creator.
My Role
Project Manager leading a team of 4 to 5 interns. Responsible for coordinating weekly check-ins, tracking deadlines, defining project scope, and keeping all work aligned with the client's goals throughout the engagement.
Challenges
The client was resistant to social media and had minimal time to invest. Additionally, she had not clearly defined who her target market was, which meant the team first had to do discovery work before a strategy could be built. Any approach that required heavy ongoing effort or was misaligned with her audience would fail from the start.
Approach
Conducted multi-source audience research analyzing social media analytics, website data, location demographics, art style, and buyer profiles. Used an iterative, agile approach — running weekly check-ins to review progress, gather feedback, and adjust direction before producing 12 original content samples aligned with her brand. Designed a bulk content system to minimize the client's ongoing time investment.
Tools Used
Google Docs Google Calendar Social Analytics Canva Zoom Instagram
Impact
✓ Final strategy presented directly to the business owner and received as well-executed
✓ Strategy designed for long-term sustainability, not just one-time use
Key Challenge Overcome
Designing an effective growth strategy for a client who was resistant to social media required creative problem solving. We focused on simplicity, batching, and authenticity to make the plan one she could realistically maintain long-term.

Business Launch & Operations

Entrepreneurship WasteAway LLC Co-Founder & Ops Lead 3 Months · 2-Person Team
+
Project Overview
Launched a junk removal and dumpster rental business from the ground up within a $15,000 startup budget in a highly competitive local market. The goal was to go from concept to fully operational with paying customers within 3 months.
My Role
Co-Founder and Operations Lead responsible for all back-office functions: market research, competitive analysis, financial planning, vendor negotiations, legal coordination, systems design, website creation, and social media setup. Defined all project goals, milestones, and deadlines.
Challenges
Entering a saturated market on a tight budget with no existing brand recognition. Paid advertising was not financially viable, so the business needed a creative path to its first customers without overspending.
Approach
Took a hybrid approach — following a waterfall structure for the sequential operational setup (legal, banking, systems, website) while staying agile in the go-to-market strategy, iterating on community engagement tactics based on early customer response. Maintained a job tracking system to monitor profitability and lead sources, enabling data-informed decisions from day one.
Tools Used
Excel Google Workspace Notion Slack Nextdoor Squarespace AI Tools
Impact
✓ Fully operational LLC launched on schedule within $15,000 budget
✓ First paying customer acquired within Week 1 of opening
✓ Built customer base without paid advertising spend
Key Challenge Overcome
Differentiating a new business in a saturated market on a tight budget required creative, research-driven strategy. By prioritizing community presence over paid advertising, we built trust and a customer base without overspending.

Wedding Planning & Vendor Management

Personal Project Lead Planner 11 Months · 50 Guests
+
Project Overview
Planned and executed an intimate 50-guest wedding within a strict $20,000 budget over 11 months. With 10+ vendors to coordinate and a non-negotiable event date, the project required careful scheduling, budget management, and proactive risk planning from day one.
My Role
Sole project lead responsible for vendor sourcing and negotiation, contract management, budget tracking, timeline creation, and full day-of coordination across venue, catering, photography, attire, alterations, and bar service.
Challenges
Three months before the wedding, the primary food vendor fell through entirely — requiring a complete catering pivot under significant time pressure, without any flexibility on the date or budget.
Approach
Applied a waterfall methodology — mapping out sequential phases from venue and catering to attire and day-of logistics, with each stage building on the last toward the fixed event date. Built and maintained master tracking spreadsheets for budget and all vendor timelines. When the vendor fell through, quickly sourced a replacement and restructured the catering concept from scratch, keeping all other workstreams on track throughout the pivot.
Tools Used
Excel Google Sheets Trello Notion AI Tools
Impact
✓ Delivered on time and within $20,000 budget
✓ Managed 10+ vendor relationships to successful completion
✓ Navigated major vendor failure 3 months out with zero timeline or budget impact
Key Challenge Overcome
Six months before the wedding, the decision was made to change venues entirely in order to secure a location with more desirable add-ons. Managed the full transition, renegotiating contracts and realigning all vendor logistics around the new location without impacting the timeline or exceeding the budget.
